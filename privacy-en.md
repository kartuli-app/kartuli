# Privacy Notice

## Summary

kartuli.app is a Georgian learning app designed to work without an account.

This notice describes the intended privacy behavior for the app.

## What is always stored on your device

kartuli.app uses a small amount of essential client-side storage so the app can work as intended.

This may include:
- your selected UI language
- your privacy choice
- local learning progress, such as item activity or view counts
- anonymous local identifiers used to keep your on-device learning state consistent

This essential storage is part of app functionality. It does not mean optional analytics are enabled.

## Optional analytics

kartuli.app treats analytics as optional.

The app uses 3 privacy states:
- `Not chosen yet`
- `Accepted`
- `Rejected`

Analytics behavior:
- if your choice is `Not chosen yet`, analytics stay off
- if your choice is `Accepted`, analytics may be sent
- if your choice is `Rejected`, analytics stay off

kartuli.app does not use a cookieless analytics fallback.

## Privacy banner

When the app starts and no privacy choice has been stored yet, the app shows a small persistent privacy banner.

The banner includes:
- an `Accept` action
- a `Reject` action
- a link to this privacy notice

The banner is not dismissible. It stays visible until you make a choice.

## Changing your choice

You can review and change your privacy choice later in Settings.

Changing your choice affects future analytics behavior:
- if you accept, optional analytics may start from that point forward
- if you reject, optional analytics should stop for future events

## No account

The learning experience does not require an account.

Your learning progress is stored locally on your device unless a future version introduces account-based sync.

## Updates

This notice may be updated as the product evolves.
